A man who has dedicated his life to the literary craft

Cormac McCarthy's Life

  • 1933 - Born in Providence, Rhode Island, US
  • 1937 - Relocated with his parents and six siblings to Knoxville, Tennessee
  • 1951 - Started at the University of Tennessee
  • 1952 - Dropped out from the University of Tennessee
  • 1957 - Returned to the the University of Tennessee
  • 1959 - Dropped out from the university again and never returned
  • 1959 - Received the Ingram Merrill Award for creative writing
  • 1960 - Received again the Ingram Merrill Award for creative writing
  • 1960 - For purposes of his writing career, McCarthy decided to change his first name from Charles to Cormac
  • 1965 - Published his first novel "The Orchard Keeper"
  • 1992 - Finally made a literaly breakthrough with his novel "All The Pretty Horses"
  • 2007 - Received the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction for his novel "The Road"
